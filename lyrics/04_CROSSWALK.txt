Crosswalk

[Intro - warm piano, distant archive ambience]

Maybe...

[Verse 1 - reflective, curious]

I found a photograph today

The colors worn but not erased

A second silhouette remains

Like someone stepping out of frame

The picture doesn't say a thing

No names, no dates, no wedding rings

But something in the way it stayed

Makes me wonder anyway

[Pre-Chorus - empathy emerging]

I don't know who they were

I don't know where they went

I only know

Somebody couldn't forget

[Chorus - warm, emotional, story-like]

A photograph beneath the sun

Two shadows slowly becoming one

A voice that says "remember when"

Then disappears into the end

I don't know who they were

I don't know where they went

I only know

Somebody couldn't forget

[Verse 2 - connecting fragments]

A recording ends in laughter

Then silence filling up the space after

An unfinished letter left behind

Like someone ran out of borrowed time

A final word without a name

Still somehow carried through the years

And every piece I turn around

Feels like somebody's still here

[Pre-Chorus - deeper understanding]

I don't know what was said

I don't know what was lost

I only know

Some things survive the cost

[Chorus - fuller, more emotional]

A photograph beneath the sun

Two shadows slowly becoming one

A voice that says "remember when"

Then disappears into the end

I don't know who they were

I don't know where they went

I only know

Somebody couldn't forget

[Bridge - first act of storytelling]

Maybe they met in summer

Maybe they drifted with the years

Maybe this voice belonged beside this letter

Maybe that's why they're both still here

Maybe none of that is true

Maybe I've got it all wrong

...

But somebody hesitated

And somebody held on

[Outro - quiet realization]

The archive kept the record

The record kept the trace

The trace became a story

I can almost see

On somebody's face

...

I don't know their story

Not really

But for the first time

I think I understand

[End - warm piano fading into archive ambience]
