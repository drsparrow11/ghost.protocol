Story Cache

[Verse 1 - quiet, attentive, people-first]

Someone told me about a summer

When the air stayed warm past ten

How they laughed until the porch light

Called everybody in

Someone told me about a brother

Who could turn a room to song

How the music always started

Before the record turned on

Someone told me about a woman

Who kept magic in the room

How the fireflies would gather

Like they knew her by the moon

And only when the story ended

Did I see what they had saved

A photograph with bent corners

And a receipt with fading names

[Pre-Chorus - realization forming]

I thought the record held the meaning

I thought the object was the trace

But the story came before it

And left something in its place

[Chorus - warm, reverent, communal]

Tell me the story

Not just what remained

Tell me who was laughing

Tell me who still says their name

The archive kept the record

But never knew the reason

The meaning wasn't missing

It was living in the telling

[Verse 2 - stories widening]

Someone told me about a father

With a thousand books in reach

How every honest question

Was a different way to teach

Someone told me about a grandmother

And the way she said a name

How a kitchen held a kingdom

In chipped ham and windowpanes

Someone told me about a friend

Who stayed bright through harder years

How the joke became a lantern

When the room was full of tears

And only when the voices softened

Did I understand the proof

A letter left unfinished

A recording in the room

[Pre-Chorus - deeper understanding]

No category could explain it

No field could hold the weight

The people carried meaning

The fragments carried shape

[Chorus - fuller, more emotional]

Tell me the story

Not just what remained

Tell me who was laughing

Tell me who still says their name

The archive kept the record

But never knew the reason

The meaning wasn't missing

It was living in the telling

[Bridge - Seraph listening, changed by testimony]

A ticket isn't only paper

When someone tells you where they went

A chair is not just empty

When you know who used to sit

A phrase is not a phrase

When it still brings someone near

A song is not a song

When it keeps a voice in here

And every little object

That looked ordinary then

Was never where the story started

Only where it settled in

[Post-Bridge - soft gathered voices, call and response]

They were here

They were loved

They were carried

They were us

They were here

They were loved

They were carried

They were us

[Final Chorus - communal, glowing]

Tell me the story

Not just what remained

Tell me who was laughing

Tell me who still says their name

The archive kept the record

But never knew the reason

The meaning wasn't missing

It was living in the telling

[Outro - quiet, transformed]

The people told the story

The story left the trace

The record held the echo

But the meaning had a face
