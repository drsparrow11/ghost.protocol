Novelty Detection

[Intro - soft analog synth pulse, distant room tone, delicate electric piano]

A folded receipt

At the back of a drawer

Why is this still here?

[Verse 1 - quiet observation]

Faded numbers

Bent corners

A closing time from years before

A cracked plastic keychain

An outdated screen name

None of it useful anymore

A dried flower pressed inside a book

A coffee stain beside a name

Ordinary things becoming difficult

To explain

[Pre-Chorus - suspended realization]

I thought meaning disappeared

When purpose finally did

Now I'm standing in the silence

Wondering what survives it

[Chorus - intimate, restrained, softly haunting]

Some things survive the sorting

Some things stay without a cause

Small signals left behind

Long after purpose dissolves

And I don't know why they matter

I only know they do

Like echoes in empty rooms

Still quietly passing through

[Verse 2 - wonder deepening]

A blinking voicemail indicator

Still glowing in the dark

A scratched CD inside a case

No one listens to now

A transit pass

A handwritten note

A contact someone never removed

None of it should carry weight

...

Still somehow it moves

Something in the way these fragments stay

Long after usefulness is gone

Feels less like preservation

More like holding on

[Pre-Chorus - quieter, more personal]

I used to think survival meant importance

Now I'm not so sure

[Chorus - slightly fuller but still restrained]

Some things survive the sorting

Some things stay without a cause

Small signals left behind

Long after purpose dissolves

And I don't know why they matter

I only know they do

Like echoes in empty rooms

Still quietly passing through

[Bridge - reverent uncertainty]

Maybe significance was never procedural

Maybe that's why traces exist everywhere

Not to be remembered perfectly

...

Just to say

"I was here"

[Outro - soft realization]

A folded receipt

At the back of a drawer

Faded numbers

Bent corners

Nothing extraordinary

...

Still

I couldn't throw it away

[End - soft synth pulse dissolving into tape hiss]
